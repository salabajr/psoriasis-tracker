**Note type:** Office visit — new patient evaluation
**Patient:** Daniel Okafor, 34M, MRN 4471822

## Chief Complaint
Scaly, itchy plaques on elbows, knees, and lower back, present approximately 18 months, gradually enlarging.

## History of Present Illness
Mr. Okafor is a 34-year-old man referred by his PCP for evaluation of chronic scaly plaques. Lesions began on the extensor elbows roughly a year and a half ago and have slowly spread to the knees and lumbosacral area. He describes moderate pruritus, worse in winter and after hot showers. Over-the-counter hydrocortisone 1% and emollients gave partial, temporary relief. No joint pain, morning stiffness, or dactylitis. No recent streptococcal infection. Family history notable for psoriasis in his father. Nonsmoker, alcohol 1-2 drinks/week. No new medications; specifically no beta-blockers, lithium, or antimalarials.

## Review of Systems
Negative for fevers, weight loss, arthralgias, ocular symptoms, GI complaints. Positive for pruritus as above and some embarrassment at the gym affecting how often he exercises.

## Exam
Well-appearing man in no distress. Skin: well-demarcated erythematous plaques with silvery-white micaceous scale over bilateral extensor elbows (3-5 cm), bilateral knees, and a 7 cm plaque over the sacrum. Auspitz sign present. Fingernails and toenails clear. Scalp clear. No inverse or palmoplantar lesions. Joints: no synovitis, full range of motion.

## Assessment
Chronic plaque psoriasis, moderate. Baseline severity scoring today: PASI 6.1, BSA 8%, IGA 2 (mild-to-moderate plaque elevation and scale). Quality-of-life impact assessed with the Dermatology Life Quality Index: DLQI 7, driven by itch, clothing choices, and self-consciousness during exercise.

## Plan
1. Clobetasol propionate 0.05% ointment — apply to plaques twice daily, 2 weeks on / 1 week off cycling; counseled on atrophy risk and to avoid face/folds.
2. Narrowband UVB (NB-UVB) phototherapy 3x/wk in our light unit; starting dose per skin type III protocol with standard incremental titration. First session scheduled this week.
3. Thick emollient (petrolatum-based) twice daily to entire affected area.
4. Counseled on chronicity, trigger avoidance (skin trauma, stress, heavy alcohol), and photographs taken for the chart.
5. Return to clinic in 6 weeks to reassess PASI/BSA/IGA and DLQI; sooner for any pustulation, fevers, or rapidly spreading lesions.
