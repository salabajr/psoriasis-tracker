## Interval History
Seen ahead of his routine interval for worsening skin. Now approximately eight months on adalimumab 40 mg every 2 weeks, previously well controlled (PASI 6.3 on 2026-03-16). He describes clear interval worsening over the past several weeks: existing plaques have thickened and enlarged, and he has developed new lesions on the trunk and anterior shins. ## Exam
Thick, well-demarcated erythematous plaques with heavy adherent scale over bilateral extensor elbows and knees, now confluent over the sacrum with extension to the flanks. New guttate-to-nummular plaques scattered over the upper back, abdomen, and anterior shins. No pustules. No synovitis; joints with full painless range of motion.

## Scores and Labs
Severity today: PASI 14.2, BSA 15%, IGA 3. This is a marked change from 2026-03-16 (PASI 6.3) and 2026-02-02 (PASI 6.1). DLQI 15 today (was 8), with high scores on symptoms, feelings, and work domains.

Labs drawn today, resulted same day:
- CRP 9 mg/L (ref < 5)
- ESR 24 mm/hr (ref < 15)
- CBC, CMP within normal limits

## Assessment
Chronic plaque psoriasis with significant objective progression while prescribed adalimumab — concerning for waning biologic response (possible anti-drug antibodies) if progression continues. No clinical signs of infection; no new photosensitizing or exacerbating medications reported.

## Plan
1. Continue adalimumab 40 mg subcutaneously every 2 weeks per current prescription.
2. Intensify topicals: clobetasol 0.05% ointment daily cycling to thickest plaques; emollients ongoing.
3. Repeat CRP/ESR at next visit.
4. Return to clinic in 6 weeks to reassess PASI/BSA/IGA and DLQI. If objective progression continues on adalimumab, will discuss switching biologic agents and initiate the prior-authorization workup at that visit.
